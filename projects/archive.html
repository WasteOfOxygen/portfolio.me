// 01 — OVERVIEW

This archive collects five years of selected client work across three disciplines: print, screen-based design, and environmental/spatial applications. Each project was chosen for its role in shaping the working methodology I use today.

The clients span cultural institutions, independent brands, and technology startups — from a contemporary art museum in Vienna to a fintech startup in Warsaw. The diversity was intentional: working across contexts sharpens the ability to read an audience and adjust visual language accordingly.

What connects everything here is a commitment to systems thinking — designing frameworks that scale, rather than one-off solutions that don't survive contact with real-world constraints.

// 03 — METHODOLOGY

PRINCIPLE 01 SYSTEMS FIRST Every project starts with a system, not a single artefact. Rules before exceptions. Structure before decoration.
PRINCIPLE 02 CONTEXT READING Visual language is borrowed from context. A museum identity and a fintech app demand fundamentally different vocabularies.
PRINCIPLE 03 CONSTRAINT AS TOOL Budget, timeline, and technical limits are design parameters, not obstacles. The best solutions often come from the tightest constraints.
PRINCIPLE 04 HANDOFF QUALITY A design is only as good as its implementation. Thorough documentation, clear specs, and close collaboration with production partners.
