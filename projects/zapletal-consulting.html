// 01 — OVERVIEW

Zapletal Consulting needed a PF card that could work as both a physical printed piece and a digital greeting — sent over email and social. The challenge was designing something that felt considered and personal rather than generic, while staying true to the professional nature of a consulting brand.

The design needed to translate cleanly across two very different contexts: the tactile constraints of print (paper, ink, format) and the screen-based world of digital distribution. One visual language, two outputs.

The result is a clean, typographically led card that carries the brand without overpowering it — restrained enough to feel professional, warm enough to feel genuine.

// 03 — PROCESS

STEP 01 BRIEF & CONTEXT Understood the tone the client wanted — professional but warm, not corporate. Mapped the use cases: physical cards sent to key contacts, digital version for broader distribution.
STEP 02 FORMAT DECISIONS Settled on a standard print format for the physical card. For digital, adapted proportions for screen — wider aspect ratio, adjusted type sizes for on-screen legibility.
STEP 03 DESIGN & REFINEMENT Typography-first approach. Kept decoration minimal — the hierarchy of the message carries the design. Iterated on spacing and weight until both formats felt balanced.
STEP 04 DELIVERY Delivered press-ready PDF for print and a high-resolution PNG/JPG for digital use. Included an editable version for future years.
