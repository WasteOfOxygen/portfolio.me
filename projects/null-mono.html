NULL MONO

A custom monospaced typeface designed for technical documentation and code-adjacent interfaces — 4 weights, 480 glyphs, full Latin coverage.

// 01 — OVERVIEW

Null Mono began as a personal tool — a typeface I could use across my own projects where existing monospaced options felt either too engineered or too neutral. Over fourteen months it grew into a fully released typeface with four weights and 480 glyphs per weight.

The design brief I set myself was strict: the Regular weight must perform at 9px on screen without hinting artifacts, punctuation must be immediately legible in dense code contexts, and the character set must cover all Western European languages plus a full technical symbol range.

The four weights — Light, Regular, Medium, Bold — are optically compensated rather than mechanically interpolated, giving each weight its own rhythm while maintaining a coherent family feel.

// 03 — PROCESS

STEP 01 RESEARCH Studied 30 monospaced typefaces in detail. Measured x-heights, stem widths, punctuation width, and character differentiation on low-res screens.
STEP 02 SKELETON DRAWING All 480 Regular glyphs drawn by hand on a 9×9 grid before moving to Glyphs. Skeleton approval before any weight variation.
STEP 03 WEIGHT INTERPOLATION Light and Bold drawn as masters; Medium and Regular optically corrected from the interpolation axis rather than accepted as-is.
STEP 04 TESTING + RELEASE Beta tested across six editors and five operating systems. Kerning refined through 200 test strings. Released in OTF and TTF formats.
